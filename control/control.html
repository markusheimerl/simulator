Spatial Control

State Representation

The three-dimensional state vector, \(X\), entirely describes the drone's state at a point in time.

\( X = [x, y, z, \phi, \theta, \psi, \dot{x}, \dot{y}, \dot{z}, \dot{\hat\phi}, \dot{\hat\theta}, \dot{\hat\psi}] \)

where

  • \(x, y, z\) denote the drone's positional coordinates in space,
  • \(\dot{x}, \dot{y}, \dot{z}\) represent linear velocities along the x, y, and z axes,
  • \(\phi, \theta, \psi\) stand for rotational angles around the x, y, and z axes,
  • \(\dot{\hat\phi}, \dot{\hat\theta}, \dot{\hat\psi}\) indicate rotational velocities within the drone's frame of reference.

A counterclockwise rotation of any vector around the \(x\), \(y\) or \(z\)-axis by some angle \(\alpha\) is done through multiplying it from the right to \(R_x(\alpha)\), \(R_y(\alpha)\) or \(R_z(\alpha)\).

\[ \begin{array}{cc} R_x(\alpha) = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & \cos(\alpha) & -\sin(\alpha) \\ 0 & \sin(\alpha) & \cos(\alpha) \\ \end{bmatrix} & R_y(\alpha) = \begin{bmatrix} \cos(\alpha) & 0 & \sin(\alpha) \\ 0 & 1 & 0 \\ -\sin(\alpha) & 0 & \cos(\alpha) \\ \end{bmatrix} \end{array} \] \[ R_z(\alpha) = \begin{bmatrix} \cos(\alpha) & -\sin(\alpha) & 0 \\ \sin(\alpha) & \cos(\alpha) & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 \\ \end{bmatrix} \]

Control Formulation

Comprehensive Control Formulation for Co-Axial Rotored Drones

The control of co-axial rotored drones integrates advanced principles from aerodynamics, mechanics, and control theory. Precise control algorithms, employing a blend of Proportional-Integral-Derivative (PID) controllers, Model Predictive Control (MPC), and state-space representations, are crucial for dynamic stability and responsive navigation.

The PID controller framework dynamically adjusts rotor speeds to maintain stability and respond to control inputs. This is mathematically expressed as: \[ \mathbf{T} = \mathbf{K}_p \cdot \mathbf{e} + \mathbf{K}_i \int \mathbf{e} \, dt + \mathbf{K}_d \frac{d\mathbf{e}}{dt} \] where \( \mathbf{T} \) is the thrust vector, and \( \mathbf{e} \) is the error vector.

Advanced MPC algorithms optimize control inputs over a future time horizon, formulating the optimization problem as: \[ \begin{align*} \min_{U} \quad & \sum_{k=0}^{N-1} (X_k - X_{ref})^T Q (X_k - X_{ref}) + U_k^T R U_k \\ \textrm{s.t.} \quad & X_{k+1} = AX_k + BU_k, \\ & X_{min} \leq X_k \leq X_{max}, \\ & U_{min} \leq U_k \leq U_{max}, \end{align*} \]

Additionally, state-space models are utilized to describe the system dynamics with first-order differential equations: \[ \dot{X} = AX + BU \] \[ Y = CX + DU \]

Advanced Rotor Speed Regulation

Rotor speed regulation is vital for precise control and agility. This involves a continuous adjustment process, translating high-level control inputs into precise rotor speed adjustments. The adjustment algorithm for each rotor can be expressed as: \[ \omega_i = \sqrt{\frac{T_i + D_i}{k_f}} \] where \(\omega_i\) is the angular velocity, \(T_i\) the thrust force, \(D_i\) the drag force, and \(k_f\) the lift constant.

The control system continuously optimizes rotor speeds to balance the drone, considering factors such as desired altitude, attitude, and directional control. This involves complex calculations to maintain equilibrium and efficient power usage, ensuring responsive and agile flight characteristics under various conditions.
