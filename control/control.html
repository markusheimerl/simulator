Introduction

Spatial Control

State Representation

The three-dimensional state vector, \(X\), entirely describes the drone's state at a point in time.

\( X = [x, y, z, \phi, \theta, \psi, \dot{x}, \dot{y}, \dot{z}, \dot{\hat\phi}, \dot{\hat\theta}, \dot{\hat\psi}] \)

where

  • \(x, y, z\) denote the drone's positional coordinates in space,
  • \(\dot{x}, \dot{y}, \dot{z}\) represent linear velocities along the x, y, and z axes,
  • \(\phi, \theta, \psi\) stand for rotational angles around the x, y, and z axes,
  • \(\dot{\hat\phi}, \dot{\hat\theta}, \dot{\hat\psi}\) indicate rotational velocities within the drone's frame of reference.

A counterclockwise rotation of any vector around the \(x\), \(y\) or \(z\)-axis by some angle \(\alpha\) is done through multiplying it from the right to \(R_x(\alpha)\), \(R_y(\alpha)\) or \(R_z(\alpha)\).

\[ \begin{array}{cc} R_x(\alpha) = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & \cos(\alpha) & -\sin(\alpha) \\ 0 & \sin(\alpha) & \cos(\alpha) \\ \end{bmatrix} & R_y(\alpha) = \begin{bmatrix} \cos(\alpha) & 0 & \sin(\alpha) \\ 0 & 1 & 0 \\ -\sin(\alpha) & 0 & \cos(\alpha) \\ \end{bmatrix} \end{array} \] \[ R_z(\alpha) = \begin{bmatrix} \cos(\alpha) & -\sin(\alpha) & 0 \\ \sin(\alpha) & \cos(\alpha) & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 \\ \end{bmatrix} \]

Control Formulation

The control of co-axial rotored drones involves an integration of advanced control theories, including Proportional-Integral-Derivative (PID) controllers, state-space representation, and non-linear control theories, to achieve precise stability and maneuverability. These formulations are foundational in addressing the non-linear dynamics of the drone as represented by the Newton-Euler formulation. Advanced algorithms like PID, state feedback control, or adaptive control strategies like Model Predictive Control (MPC) are implemented to maintain stability and desired flight dynamics.

The general PID formula across different approaches is given by: \[ u(t) = K_p e(t) + K_i \int_{0}^{t} e(\tau) d\tau + K_d \frac{d}{dt} e(t) \] where \( u(t) \) is the control input, \( e(t) \) is the error signal, and \( K_p, K_i, K_d \) are the proportional, integral, and derivative gains, respectively.

For a more predictive and adaptive approach, the control system can be represented using state-space models as: \[ \dot{X} = AX + BU \] \[ Y = CX + DU \] where \(X\) is the state vector, \(U\) is the control input vector, \(Y\) is the output vector, and \(A\), \(B\), \(C\), and \(D\) are system matrices.

Rotor Speed Update

Rotor speed updates are essential for the drone's flight control mechanism, translating high-level control inputs into precise rotor speed adjustments. The thrust and torque generated by each rotor are critical in this aspect and can be described by: \[ F_i = k_f \omega_i^2, \quad M_i = k_m \omega_i^2 \] where \( F_i \) and \( M_i \) are the thrust and torque generated by rotor \( i \), \( \omega_i \) is the rotor speed, and \( k_f, k_m \) are thrust and torque constants.

Additionally, the rotor speed update mechanism can be expressed as: \[ \omega_i = \sqrt{\frac{T_i + D_i}{k_f}} \] where \(\omega_i\) is the angular velocity of the i-th rotor, \(T_i\) is the thrust force, \(D_i\) is the drag force, and \(k_f\) is the lift constant.
