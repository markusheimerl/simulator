Spatial Control

State Representation

The three-dimensional state vector, \(X\), entirely describes the drone's state at a point in time.

\( X = [x, y, z, \phi, \theta, \psi, \dot{x}, \dot{y}, \dot{z}, \dot{\hat\phi}, \dot{\hat\theta}, \dot{\hat\psi}] \)

where

  • \(x, y, z\) denote the drone's positional coordinates in space,
  • \(\dot{x}, \dot{y}, \dot{z}\) represent linear velocities along the x, y, and z axes,
  • \(\phi, \theta, \psi\) stand for rotational angles around the x, y, and z axes,
  • \(\dot{\hat\phi}, \dot{\hat\theta}, \dot{\hat\psi}\) indicate rotational velocities within the drone's frame of reference.

A counterclockwise rotation of any vector around the \(x\), \(y\) or \(z\)-axis by some angle \(\alpha\) is done through multiplying it from the right to \(R_x(\alpha)\), \(R_y(\alpha)\) or \(R_z(\alpha)\).

\[ \begin{array}{cc} R_x(\alpha) = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & \cos(\alpha) & -\sin(\alpha) \\ 0 & \sin(\alpha) & \cos(\alpha) \\ \end{bmatrix} & R_y(\alpha) = \begin{bmatrix} \cos(\alpha) & 0 & \sin(\alpha) \\ 0 & 1 & 0 \\ -\sin(\alpha) & 0 & \cos(\alpha) \\ \end{bmatrix} \end{array} \] \[ R_z(\alpha) = \begin{bmatrix} \cos(\alpha) & -\sin(\alpha) & 0 \\ \sin(\alpha) & \cos(\alpha) & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 \\ \end{bmatrix} \]

Comprehensive Control Formulation and Rotor Speed Update for Co-Axial Rotored Drones

Control of co-axial rotored drones encompasses various sophisticated techniques, from basic PID controllers to advanced algorithms like Model Predictive Control (MPC) and non-linear control theories. These methods are essential in addressing the multi-variable nonlinear problem of drone control, taking into account factors such as drone's inertia, aerodynamics, external forces, and desired flight path.

The PID control mechanism, a common approach across different versions, is defined as:

\( u(t) = K_p e(t) + K_i \int_{0}^{t} e(\tau) d\tau + K_d \frac{d}{dt} e(t) \),

where \( u(t) \) is the control input, \( e(t) \) is the error signal, and \( K_p, K_i, K_d \) are the proportional, integral, and derivative gains, respectively. This control adjusts rotor speeds based on the deviation from the desired state, considering proportional error, accumulated error, and the rate of error change.

For more complex scenarios, MPC optimizes control inputs over a future time horizon, expressed as:

\[ \begin{align*} \min_{U} \quad & \sum_{k=0}^{N-1} (X_k - X_{ref})^T Q (X_k - X_{ref}) + U_k^T R U_k \\ \textrm{s.t.} \quad & X_{k+1} = AX_k + BU_k, \\ & X_{min} \leq X_k \leq X_{max}, \\ & U_{min} \leq U_k \leq U_{max}, \end{align*} \]

Rotor speed updates are crucial for achieving desired thrust and torque. The process is governed by equations of motion for each rotor, where the thrust \( F_i \) and torque \( M_i \) are a function of rotor speed \( \omega_i \) and constants \( k_f \) and \( k_m \). The control system computes the optimal rotor speeds by solving the inverse dynamics problem, ensuring accurate and responsive changes to external commands and environmental factors.

\( F_i = k_f \omega_i^2, \quad M_i = k_m \omega_i^2 \)

The integration of these advanced control and feedback mechanisms, including state-space models and electronic speed controllers (ESCs), allows the drone to perform complex tasks with high precision, adapting fluidly to changing environmental conditions and operational demands.
